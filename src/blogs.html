The Potential of Towels - 11/07/2019

“A towel, [The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy] says, is about the most massively useful thing an interstellar hitchhiker can have. Partly it has great practical value. You can wrap it around you for warmth as you bound across the cold moons of Jaglan Beta; you can lie on it on the brilliant marble-sanded beaches of Santraginus V, inhaling the heady sea vapors; you can sleep under it beneath the stars which shine so redly on the desert world of Kakrafoon; use it to sail a miniraft down the slow heavy River Moth; wet it for use in hand-to-hand-combat; wrap it round your head to ward off noxious fumes or avoid the gaze of the Ravenous Bugblatter Beast of Traal (such a mind-boggingly stupid animal, it assumes that if you can't see it, it can't see you); you can wave your towel in emergencies as a distress signal, and of course dry yourself off with it if it still seems to be clean enough.”

Fun and Sandcastles - 12/04/2023

If you’ve read my resume on the ‘My Story’ page, you may have noticed that I’ve jumped around a few career paths. Not necessarily very many, but certainly in directions very polar to one another: science, recruitment, finance, hospitality, and now look at me - web development!
It may very well be too soon to tell, but I’ve noticed something in this that I haven't in many of the other career attempts: this is kind of fun. And by that i dont mean its fun how easy it is, by god it is intense, challenging and frustrating to no end: To see your page you worked so hard towards become scrambled, and destroyed after one misplaced semicolon is very daunting. But it's also rewarding in its own way: to get to a point that you see your page scrambled, but to be able to look at the muddled components and to be able to decipher what is tripping over what. And then to make changes with intention and belief that those changes would restore things. And then to see the elements behave as you had wished them to… it's satisfying, and kind of like magic.
Learning the potential of being able to do so much instils a similar enthusiasm, curiosity and inquisitiveness that I can only describe as ‘a twelve-year-old discovers the transitions in PowerPoint.’ suddenly you want to implement them all: have your text glow, and spin and fly, make the background your favourite gif, put a link to a rickroll (have I put one in this website or havent I?). I guess another way to describe it is being in a sandbox, but in this one you gotta remember to put your semicolons or your sandcastle will crumble.
I hope that I can continue to engage with this path as I have with this website: The stress, the frustration, the fun and the magic.

- Arda Erinc Oguz
